University of Aberdeen Postgraduate Seminar Series (Postgrad. MathClub) 2011

Each talk is held on Tuesday around the time slot 12 - 3pm. This is a provisional schedule:

Date Time Speaker Title
1st March 12-1pm Andrew Russhard Introduction to the Serre Spectral Sequence - Slides
In this talk I will give the method of constructing the Serre spectral sequence for homology (I'll point out the obvious dual for cohomology too) and state (but not prove because it's too long) the convergence theorem for the spectral sequence. Then I will give a theorem of Serre which gives a nice way of representing the \(E_2\) page for spaces \(X\) where we have a fibration \(F \rightarrow X \rightarrow B\), and work through one (or possibly two) example(s) of how we can use the spectral sequences.

I may also refer to a couple of books for the proofs:
McCleary - A User's Guide to Spectral Sequences
Mosher & Tangora - Cohomology Operations and Applications in Homotopy Theory.
8th March 11.30-12.30pm Aaron Chan Quivers, their representations and applications - Slides
This is an expository talk on quivers divided into four sections: (1) Intro. to quivers (2) Ext-quiver and results (3) Auslander-Reiten theory (4) Examples.
We assume a basic knowledge of algebras and their representations (projective, injective, iindecomposable and simple modules) and we will use algebraic and combinatorial arguments throughout (modulo a small amount of geometry in sections 2 and 3).

The standard reference to these material are:
[ARS] Auslander,Reiten,Smalo - Representation Theory of Artin Algebras
[ASS] Assem, Simson, Skowronski - Elements of Representation Theory of Associative Algebras Volume 1
[B] Benson - Representations and Cohomology volume 1
15th March 1-2pm Fergus Reid Varieties for Modules over Elementary Abelian p-groups - Slides
I will give a brief overview on varieties for modules, particularly modules over elementary abelian \(p\)-groups. I will say a little about in what sense a module can be said to have a variety and then proceed to describe rank varieties for modules over elementary abelian \(p\)-groups, showing how these relate to the previously defined varieties for modules. This done I shall give some examples of how using rank varieties can make it a great deal easier to explicitly calculate the variety of a module in this situation. If there is time I will finish by saying a short amount about what I'm working on at the minute relating to these.

In terms of assumed knowledge I'll expect everybody to know basic cohomology (essentially the definition of Ext should be enough to see you through) and the definition of a variety. I'll have to make use of some group cohomology but I'll try and quickly state what I need and include references for further information. If you know in advance how the cohomology of a group can be made into a ring that would certainly help but shouldn't be essential.
