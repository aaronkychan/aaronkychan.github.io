2020-10-27 (Tue) 1630-1800 Japan Time

  • Title: Positive cluster complex and \tau-tilting complex

  • Speaker: Yasuaki Gyoda 行田 康晃

  • Abstract: In cluster algebra theory, cluster complexes are actively studied as simplicial complexes, which represent the structure of a seed and its mutations. In this talk, I will discuss a certain subcomplex of a cluster complex, which is called positive cluster complex. This is a subcomplex whose vertex set consists of all cluster variables except for those in the initial seed. I will also introduce another simplicial complex in this talk - the tau-tilting complex. This is a simplicial complex whose vertex set consists of all indecomposable tau-rigid modules, and simplices are basic tau-rigid modules. The tau-tilting complex of a cluster-tilted algebra corresponds with some positive cluster complex. Due to this fact, we can investigate the structure of a tau-tilting complex of finite-representation type by using the tools of cluster algebra theory. This is joint work with Haruhisa Enomoto.
